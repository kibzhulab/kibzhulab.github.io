Brief Introduction:

Dr. Zhu Andan is a researcher at the Kunming Institute of Botany, Chinese Academy of Sciences and head of the Plant Genomics and Computational Biology Research Group. He has published over 25 articles in peer-reviewed journals, such as Nature Genetics, New phytologist, BMC Genomics, Molecular Biology and Evolution He obtained his doctorate in fruit tree science from Huazhong Agricultural University in 2012 and obtained his postdoctoral degree in agriculture and horticulture from the University of Nebraska at Lincoln in 2015. He served as Reasearch Fellow from December 2012 to March 2017 in Computational Biology and Bioinformatics, University of Texas at Austin. he worked as a researcher at Kunming Institute of Botany, Chinese Academy of Sciences in April 2017, and established the Plant Genomics and Computational Biology Research Group.
At present, this research group is mainly through the method of combining computational biologyand experiment, to study the diversity of wild species resources and the formation mechanism of special and important horticultural traits from the perspectives of genomic complexity, multi-polygenome interaction, gene expression regulation and gene function analysis.

Key reseach content:

1) Models and mechanisms of nucleo-plasmic co-evolution, Including the law of structural variation of plant mitochondrial genome and its relationship with variation rate and recombination, the role of nucleo-cytoplasmic interaction in seed development, etc.
2)Plant polyploid formation and expression of regulatory mechanisms, including the dose effect of ploidy variation, Cis or trans regulatory variation, And the role of epigenetic modification in important flower resources and the development seeds.
3)The collection and utilization of characteristic wild germplasm resources.
